*Training synchronization dynamics for neural operator models on deformable domains.*
Grouped bar chart illustrating the synchronization score per training iteration for two neural operator models: DIMON (blue) and the proposed GeoCMON architecture ("Ours", purple). The synchronization score quantifies the feature-level coordination and temporal consistency within each model during learning, with higher values indicating greater harmony among network branches. The x-axis denotes training iterations (1–50, shown every 5 steps), and the y-axis represents synchronization scores ranging from 0.0 to 1.1. Throughout training, GeoCMON maintains consistently elevated and stable synchronization, reflecting improved branch interaction and optimization stability compared to DIMON. This metric substantiates the effectiveness of the multi-branch disentangled design and conditional residual connections introduced in GeoCMON, contributing to more robust convergence and generalization on complex, non-rigid PDE domains. Error bars are omitted for clarity.

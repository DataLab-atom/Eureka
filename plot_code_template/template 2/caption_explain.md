**Stratified per-sample weighted loss distributions for DIMON and GeoCMON (Ours) at final training epoch across label magnitude bins.** Boxplots display the distributions of per-sample weighted loss (vertical axis; loss = $(|u|+1)\cdot \text{MSE}$) for test data divided into four bins by solution label magnitude (horizontal axis: Bin 0–Bin 3, with Bin 0 representing hardest samples and Bin 3 the easiest). The DIMON baseline (blue) is compared to the proposed GeoCMON method (pink). For each bin and method, boxes indicate interquartile range, whiskers denote 1.5×IQR, horizontal lines mark the median, white circles indicate the mean, and isolated points denote outliers. GeoCMON consistently achieves lower median and mean weighted loss, as well as reduced variance and fewer outliers, especially for bins representing high-difficulty samples. This quantifies the improved accuracy and robustness of GeoCMON in physically significant solution regimes, attributed to its disentangled architecture and magnitude-aware training objective.

Comparison of the standard deviation of gradient noise at each network layer during training. The blue curve represents our method (GeoCMON with conditional residual connections), and the orange curve represents the baseline method (DIMON). The shaded area represents the range of ±1 standard deviation. The horizontal axis represents the network layer (layer 1 to layer 4), and the vertical axis represents the magnitude of gradient noise. The results show that the gradient noise amplitude of the two methods is comparable at each layer, indicating that our method maintains comparable optimization stability to the baseline after introducing conditional residual connections and weighted loss, verifying the stability of gradient noise characteristics during model training.
